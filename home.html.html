ü§î What is a Chalk Talk?

A chalk talk is an informal 50-minute presentation where you'll discuss your research while writing/drawing on a whiteboard. For this E-TRAIN program, we're practicing with a 15-minute version to build your experience and confidence. Think of it as a conversation about your work, not a formal lecture.

Chalk Talk ‚úÖ Traditional Presentation ‚ùå
Interactive, conversational tone Formal, one-way presentation
Write/draw on whiteboard live Pre-made slides with animations
50 minutes (15 min for E-TRAIN practice) 30-60 minute formal talk
Focus on research approach Heavy emphasis on results
Demonstrate thinking process Present polished conclusions

üí° Key Insight

They want to see how you think, not just what you've accomplished. Your chalk talk should demonstrate your scientific reasoning, problem-solving approach, and ability to communicate complex ideas clearly. Our 15-minute E-TRAIN version gives you focused practice on these essential skills.

üèóÔ∏è The IMPACT Structure

IMPACT is your framework for a compelling chalk talk presentation. Each letter represents a key component that builds toward a powerful presentation. While a full chalk talk is typically 50 minutes, we're using a condensed 15-minute version for E-TRAIN training.

I
IGNITE (2 minutes)

Attention-grabbing opening hook

Start with a compelling story, statistic, or question that immediately connects your audience to why your research matters.

  • Patient story or clinical scenario
  • Surprising statistic
  • Thought-provoking question
M
MAP (2 minutes)

Problem identification and knowledge gap

Clearly define the problem you're addressing and the specific gap in current knowledge that your research fills.

  • What's the current state?
  • What's missing or unknown?
  • Why does this matter?
P
PRESENT (1 minute)

Research aims overview

Concisely present your two main research aims that address the knowledge gap you've identified.

  • Aim 1: [Brief description]
  • Aim 2: [Brief description]
  • How they connect
A
ADDRESS (4 minutes)

First aim in detail

Deep dive into your first aim with specific methods, expected outcomes, and potential challenges.

  • Detailed methodology
  • Expected results
  • Alternative approaches
C
CONTINUE (4 minutes)

Second aim in detail

Thoroughly explain your second aim, showing how it builds on or complements the first aim.

  • Connection to Aim 1
  • Unique methodology
  • Anticipated outcomes
T
TRANSLATE (2 minutes)

Broader impact and future directions

Connect your research back to the bigger picture and its potential impact on the field or patient care.

  • Clinical implications
  • Future research directions
  • Broader field impact
